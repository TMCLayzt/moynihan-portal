About the Program

About the fellowship

The Moynihan Center Fellowship at The City College of New York is a year-long academic and civic engagement program connecting undergraduate students to New York City’s political landscape.

Fellows participate in two parallel tracks: PSC 31180 — Power, Politics, and Policy in NYC (led by Layana Abu Touq) and PSC 31330 — Truth and Politics / TAP (led by Dr. Michael Miller).

Through lectures, guest speakers, field experiences, and research projects, fellows develop practical skills in policy analysis, civic participation, and professional development.

How to use this portal
1
Dashboard
Your home base. See the latest announcements from your instructors, upcoming deadlines, and the next class sessions at a glance.
2
Calendar
Browse all events by month. Click any date to see details. Use the course filter to view PSC 31180, TAP 31330, or all events. Click Add to calendar on any event to download a .ics file for Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook.
3
Syllabus
View the full course syllabus organized by module. Each module has tabs for Overview, Sessions, Deliverables, Readings, and linked Events. Switch between PSC 31180 and TAP 31330 using the course buttons.
4
RSVPs
Guest speaker events and joint fellowship events require an RSVP. You’ll see an RSVP button on those events in the calendar and syllabus. Some events also have an Eventbrite registration link.
5
Finance checklist
Track your fellowship financial documents (W-2s, FAFSA, stipend paperwork). Check off items as you complete them. Your instructor adds and manages this list.
6
Resources
Find links to important documents, forms, academic guides, housing resources, and more. Organized by category.
7
Change your password
Click your initials in the top-right corner and select Change password. Your default password was set by your instructor — change it when you first log in.
Admin guide
A
Adding students
Go to Admin → Students. Upload a CSV (columns: name, email, course) or add manually. All new students get the default password moynihan2025. They can change it after logging in.
B
Alumni / inactive students
When a fellow graduates or leaves, click Deactivate on their row in Admin → Students. They are marked as Alumni and cannot log in, but their data is preserved. Use Reactivate to restore access.
C
Events & calendar
Add events in Admin → Events. Fill in title, date, category, course, and optionally an Eventbrite URL, description, and check Required to mark mandatory attendance. You can also add events directly from the calendar by clicking any date.
D
Finance checklist
In Admin → Finance, add items like “Submit W-2” or “Complete FAFSA.” Students see these as a checklist on their Finance tab and can check off each item as they complete it.
E
Resources
In Admin → Resources, add links to documents, forms, or guides. Choose a category (General, Finance, Academic, Forms, Housing) and add a URL and description.
F
Announcements with week tags
When posting an announcement, you can add a week number. Students will see announcements filtered to the current week when you set a filter, or all announcements if no filter is active.
